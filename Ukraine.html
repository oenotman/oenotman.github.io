Introduction

Ukraine was the center of the first eastern Slavic state called Kyivan Rus. It was the most powerful state in Europe during the 10th and 11th centuries. Kyivan Rus left a cultural and religious legacy which was the foundation of Ukrainian nationalism through the centuries.

When the Czars of Russia fell out of power, Ukraine was independent for three years from 1917-1920. Unfortunately, it was conquered once again and was apart of the USSR until its collapse in 1991. Ukraine gained its independence once again.

Their independence became threatened on February 24, 2022 when Russia invaded them on many fronts. Ukraine is currently fighting for their independence against a wrongful invasion.

Location

Ukraine is located in Eastern Europe. It borders the Black Sea along with Romania, Poland and Moldava to the west. It shares borders with Russia to the east.

Its geographic coordinates are 49 00 N, and 32 00 E. Map references are Europe and Asia.

National Holiday

Ukraine celebrates its Independence Day on August 24th each year since 1991. They declared their independence from Soviet Russia after its collapse. Ukraine celebrates Unity Day because of the time when Ukrainian republics united in 1919 during its brief independence from the Czars.

Citizenship

Ukraine does not grant citizenship by birth. They only grant citizenship by descent where at least one parent has citizenship. Ukraine does not recognize dual citizenship, and requires 5 years of residency for naturalization.

Economy
Economic Overview

During the years of the USSR, Ukraine was the second most important economic component after Russia. Ukraine's fertile soil accounted for more than a quarter of agricultural output. The farms provided substantial amounts of meat, milk, grain and vegetables to other republics.

After declaring independence in August 1991, the Ukrainian government began creating a legal framework for privatization. However, there was widespread resistance to reform within the government and led to some backtracking. Output by 1999 had fallen to less than 40% of the 1991 level. Real GDP
