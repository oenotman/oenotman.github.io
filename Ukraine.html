Introduction

Ukraine was the center of the first eastern Slavic state called Kyivan Rus. It was the most powerful state in Europe during the 10th and 11th centuries. Ukraine was independent for three yearsafter the Czars were dethroned. It was conquered by the USSR until its collapse in 1991.

National Holiday

Ukraine celebrates its Independence Day on August 24th each year since 1991. They declared their independence from Soviet Russia after its collapse. Ukraine celebrates Unity Day because of the time when Ukrainian republics united in 1919 during its brief independence from the Czars.

Citizenship

Ukraine does not grant citizenship by birth. They only grant citizenship by descent where at least one parent has citizenship. Ukraine does not recognize dual citizenship, and requires 5 years of residency for naturalization.

Economy
Economic Overview

During the years of the USSR, Ukraine was the second most important economic component after Russia. Ukraine's fertile soil accounted for more than a quarter of agricultural output. After declaring independence in August 1991, the Ukrainian government began creating a legal framework for privatization.
